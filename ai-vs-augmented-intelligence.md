Artificial Intelligence vs Augmented Intelligence
1. Definition

Artificial Intelligence (AI):
AI refers to systems that are designed to simulate human intelligence and can perform tasks independently, sometimes without human involvement.

Augmented Intelligence:
Augmented Intelligence focuses on using AI to enhance human intelligence, helping humans make better decisions rather than replacing them.

2. Core Philosophy

AI: “Can machines replace humans?”

Augmented Intelligence: “How can machines help humans?”

👉 This is the biggest difference — replacement vs collaboration

3. Decision-Making

AI:

Can make decisions on its own

Example: automated trading systems, self-driving cars

Augmented Intelligence:

Only provides suggestions

Human makes the final decision

👉 Control stays with humans

4. Human Involvement

AI: Minimal or no human involvement after setup

Augmented Intelligence: Continuous human involvement

👉 Humans + AI work together

5. Use Cases Comparison
AI Use Cases:
Self-driving vehicles
Chatbots
Fraud detection systems
Voice assistants

Augmented Intelligence Use Cases:
Medical diagnosis support
Business decision support
Education analytics
Financial advisory tools

6. Risk & Trust
AI Risks:
Wrong decisions without human check
Bias in algorithms
Job replacement concerns

Augmented Intelligence Advantages:
Human validation reduces risk
More trustworthy decisions
Better ethical control
